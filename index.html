World Setting

History and background of the post-nuclear wasteland world

Back to Home

The Great Destruction

In 2077, the twilight of human civilization arrived. A global nuclear war known as "The Great Catastrophe" wiped out the civilization achievements of humanity in just seven hours. Cities turned into ruins, the earth was covered with radioactive dust, and the sky was shrouded in eternal gloom.

The nuclear winter lasted for a full twenty years. When the survivors finally emerged from their underground shelters and climbed back to the surface, they found a world completely transformed. The once prosperous cities now only had distorted steel skeletons, covered in radioactive dust and rust. The natural ecosystem had completely collapsed, replaced by all kinds of strange creatures mutated by radiation.

Fifty years later, the world is divided into several distinct regions: Old World and New World

Old World

Old World City Ruins

The ruins of the old world, where once prosperous cities stood, have completely fallen into disrepair and have been reoccupied by wild plants. Hospitals, Residential houses, school.......They are almost deserted and seem to be full of vitality compared to the new world. Here, you can search for the resources of the old world, the remaining high-tech items from the past. By making use of them, you are sure to carve out a new path in the new world.

New World

Dust

The vast desert and wasteland seem relatively safe on the surface, but in reality they are full of hidden dangers. Sandstorms, extreme temperature differences, scarce resources, and mutated creatures lurking beneath the sand dunes make traversing this area a life-and-death challenge.

Survivor Outposts

The Last Spark of Human Civilization. From rudimentary shantytowns to relatively well-structured underground shelters, these outposts are important places for survivors to conduct transactions, rest, and obtain information. However, even these "safe zones" often become targets of attackers.

In this world, the constraints of morality and law have long ceased to exist. The survivors have split into multiple factions:

New Factions, Associations and Forces on the dust

The New California Republic

An organization that attempts to restore the pre-war government system, possessing the most advanced technology and military strength, but severely hindered by bureaucracy.

The Raiders' Tribe

A savage group of survivors who make a living by engaging in violent plundering, occupying the core area of the ruined city.

The Technological Renaissance Society

An organization dedicated to restoring pre-war technologies, possessing advanced manufacturing and medical techniques.

Wilderness Wanderers

Solitary survivors who roam the wasteland relying on their knowledge of the wilderness and survival skills.

The player will struggle to survive in this harsh world, having to confront natural threats such as radiation, hunger, and disease, as well as navigate among various factions and make crucial choices that will determine their fate. Every exploration may bring valuable resources or deadly dangers; every encounter may be a life-saving hand or a hidden dagger.

This is the end times - a world where a civilization has perished, but human nature is still struggling to survive. Here, every drop of clean water is worth exchanging with blood, and every glimmer of hope may be swallowed up by endless dust in the next moment.

Hand-drawn art style

All the characters and map scenes are hand-drawn

Hand-drawn Characters
Hand-drawn maps and scenes

Unique Artistic Identity & Authenticity

  • Amplifies Traces of "Humanity and Memory" in the Apocalypse
  • Hand-drawn strokes and textures feel like charcoal sketches in a scavenged journal, carrying the warmth of a lost civilization
  • Creates a poignant contrast against cold, rigid metal ruins, screaming "people lived here."
  • Watercolor-style skies can depict toxic sunsets and radiation storms with haunting beauty
  • Ink washes and rough textures perfectly embody decay, erosion, and the slow reclaiming of nature
